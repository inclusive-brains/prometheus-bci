Methodology — How Quality is Computed

The quality score (0–100%) is computed in real-time from the filtered EEG signal using a sliding window of 256 samples (~2s at 128 Hz). Four independent criteria are evaluated and summed.

Temporal Smoothness

0 – 40 pts

Lag-1 autocorrelation of the signal. Real EEG bandpass-filtered at 0.1–40 Hz is temporally smooth: consecutive samples are highly correlated (r > 0.8). Random noise has near-zero autocorrelation.

r = Cov(x[t], x[t+1]) / Var(x) score = min(40, r × 50)
Real EEG: r ≈ 0.9 → 40 pts White noise: r ≈ 0.0 → 0 pts

High-Frequency Noise Ratio

0 – 30 pts

Compares sample-to-sample difference variance to overall signal variance. Approximates the proportion of high-frequency content without requiring FFT.

HF ratio = Var(diff(x)) / (2 × Var(x))
Clean EEG: ratio < 0.1 → 30 pts White noise: ratio ≈ 1.0 → 0 pts

Amplitude Plausibility

0 – 15 pts

Checks that the signal RMS falls within the expected range for EEG. Too low (< 0.1 µV) indicates no contact, too high (> 500 µV) suggests saturation or artifacts.

RMS = √(mean(x²))
0.5 – 150 µV → 15 pts 150 – 300 µV → 8 pts < 0.1 or > 500 → 0 pts

Signal Stability

0 – 15 pts

Crest factor: ratio of peak amplitude to RMS. Detects transient spikes and artifacts. A stable signal without large spikes scores higher.

CF = max(|x - μ|) / RMS
CF < 3 → 15 pts CF 3–5 → 10 pts CF > 8 → 0 pts

SNR Estimation

Signal-to-noise ratio is derived from the lag-1 autocorrelation coefficient, assuming an AR(1) signal model:

SNR = 10 × log10(r / (1 - r)) dB

This gives physically meaningful values: r = 0.9 → 9.5 dB, r = 0.5 → 0 dB, r < 0.05 → capped at -10 dB.
